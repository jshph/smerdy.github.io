Thinking outside the Board: Redesigning Pinterest's organization model

Pinterest goes by the name of a “visual discovery tool,” and it is good at finding content for users. It’s good at information retrieval, but perhaps it’s a signifier of underutilized potential that, seemingly by casual extension of this functionality, Pinterest serves as a mood-boarding tool. What if it allowed users to do more than just hoard content en masse into Boards? I had questions about what mood-boarding could be through Pinterest.

My Principles of Interaction Design class assigned several projects involving user studies, and I took this liberty to address this curiosity of mine. I surveyed some fellow Pinterest users, then dreamt up a paper prototype. What follows is a summary of my study and design, as well as some reflection upon writing this up a few months after the class ended.
