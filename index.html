About

I'm studying computer science at UT Austin (B.S., May 2018), also pursuing a Bridging Disciplines Program certificate in Digital Arts and Media. Nowadays, you'll probably find me working on an open data project for Convergent, designing or branding for DECO Studio, building friendships and understanding faith with Asian American Campus Ministry, or teaching a kid how to code with Code Orange. But I also enjoy thinking over Medium posts (@joshuapham), working on my puns and captionautobiography, or taking and editing photos (superfan of Snapseed, Google Photos, and Lightroom).

A couple of years ago, I transferred from De Anza College in Cupertino, CA where I helped start Developers' Guild and their chapter of InterVarsity Christian Fellowship.

Since 2014 I've been learning UX and front-end web dev, with the goal of building tools for creativity, productivity, and music (tldr: man-computer symbiosis). More recently I've been learning how to work with unstructured data (information retrieval, text analysis, and computer vision) (tldr: machine perception). Last summer, I interned at Dolby Laboratories building a spatial audio + virtual reality video plugin in C++ for Unity. This summer, I'm excited to be interning at Spotify working on their music recommendation engine + knowledge graph.

If you're interested, you can find me on GitHub @smerdy. Moodify is a project that spun out of a hackathon, where I was curious about song categorization by using lyrics instead of audio qualities. My team has documented our pipeline to identify topics in songs using distributional semantics methods, in a Google doc here.

You can find my resume here, or reach out to me at jpham AT cs DOT utexas DOT edu.
